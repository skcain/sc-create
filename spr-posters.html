Stream Protection Rule Posters

Problem

The Stream Protection Rule required a series of meetings to inform and gather comments from the public on the various aspects of regulation.

Solution

Create a series of posters that easily disseminated main aspects of the rule.

Process

Office of Surface Mining Reclamation and Enforcement (OSMRE) was working to protect streams near mining operations, and needed new visual designs to raise awareness of key issues. The graphics team was asked to create seven posters that would be used as educational tools during public meetings for the Stream Protection Rule.

I managed development for three of the posters: Protection of Hydrologic Balance, Revegetation, Soil and Fish and Wildlife Protection and Coal Regions. I worked with a policy expert in each area who ensured that the design accurately represented the new regulations.

I started by interviewing and learning from the experts and then asked them to sketch their ideas. I refined the sketches, gathered more feedback and began building in Illustrator. After managing several rounds of approval, the designs were ready for print.
